# 712 (118712.fr)

> French directory-enquiries site with a free reverse-lookup (annuaire inversé) — turn a French phone number into the listed subscriber's name and address.

## When to use
You have a French `phone` number (landline or a 06/07 mobile) and need to identify the subscriber: their `name` and `address`. Use it as the France-specific equivalent of a phonebook reverse lookup when a missing person or associate has a French number.

## How to use it (`bestInteractionPattern`: web-manual)
1. Go to https://www.118712.fr and open the reverse lookup ("Annuaire inversé gratuit" / annuaire-inverse-gratuit.html).
2. Enter the `phone` number (French format). Solve any captcha if prompted.
3. Read the result: for listed subscribers you get `name` (or business name) and `address`; for businesses you may also get activity type, hours, fax/email/website.
4. Pivot: feed the returned `name`/`address` into French people-search, electoral or social tools; cross-check with the forward directory (search by name) to confirm.

## Inputs → Outputs
- **In:** `phone` (French number)
- **Out:** `name`, `address`
- **Empty/negative result looks like:** "no result"/number not listed. Numbers on the "liste rouge" (ex-directory by request) and most pure-mobile contracts won't resolve — absence is not proof the number is fake.

## Gotchas & OpSec
- Human-in-the-loop: occasional captcha; French-language UI.
- Coverage is France only and limited to *listed* subscribers; unlisted/liste-rouge numbers are intentionally absent.
- Don't confuse the free web lookup with 118712's paid phone directory-assistance service.
- OpSec: passive; queries the published directory, no contact with the subscriber.

## Overlaps ("do both")
- Pairs with general phone tools and French people-search; for non-French numbers use country-specific directories (`[[canada411-ca]]`, `[[britishphonebook-com]]`).

## Trust & verifiability
`trust: trusted` — a licensed French directory-enquiries operator drawing on official public listings; results for listed subscribers are reliable.
