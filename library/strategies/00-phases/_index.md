# Phases

The skeleton of an investigation. Most cases move through these in order, looping back as new selectors arrive. OpSec is not a stage — it rides every phase.

`intake → triage → pivot → enrichment → verification → reporting` (with `opsec` cross-cutting)

## Children
- **[[phase-intake]]** — turn a messy "find this person" into a structured case: seed selectors with provenance, an explicit "found" definition, and the constraints (age, time window, origin platform).
- **[[phase-triage]]** — order the work by yield × uniqueness over cost; run the cheap high-value passive pivots first; flag thin-selector cases honestly.
- **[[phase-pivot]]** — the engine: grow the selector graph breadth-first, logging every edge with provenance, verification-gated.
- **[[phase-enrichment]]** — go deep on a *confirmed* node to extract every selector and fact it holds (depth-first, only safe after verification).
- **[[phase-verification]]** — continuous: gate every candidate→confirmed promotion on two independent signals; the safety rail of the whole tree.
- **[[phase-reporting]]** — package the graph into an honest, sourced, confidence-labeled handoff routed to the proper authority.
- **[[phase-opsec]]** — cross-cutting: stay passive, never tip off the subject, protect subject/investigation/investigator in that order.

## How they relate
Pivot and enrichment are the two motions — wide then deep. Verification gates the transition (you only go deep on what you've confirmed). Triage decides order; intake sets the rules; reporting closes it; opsec constrains all of it.
