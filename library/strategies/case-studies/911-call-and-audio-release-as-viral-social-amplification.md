# 911-call and audio release as viral social amplification

> Releasing Brandon Lawson's 911 call publicly sparked viral social-media spread, was picked up by a major YouTube creator (LordanARTS) and sustained over years by true-crime podcasts (True Crime Garage, Crawlspace, 2013-2021), with follow-up episodes used to correct media misconceptions. The transferable lesson: a single emotionally compelling primary artifact (audio, last video, distinctive photo) can be the engine that recruits a crowd of analysts and witnesses. Identify the one shareable artifact in your case and route it to the platforms where the relevant audience already gathers.

**When to use:** When a case has stalled and you need to recruit witnesses or crowd analysts via a compelling primary artifact.

## Steps
- Identify the single most emotionally compelling, shareable primary artifact (audio, last post, photo).
- Release it to creators/communities whose audience overlaps with potential witnesses.
- Sustain visibility through serialized coverage rather than a one-time post.
- Publish follow-ups to correct misconceptions and keep the lead funnel clean.

## Signals it's working
- Organic shares and new tips spike after release
- Creators pick up and re-amplify the artifact

## Pitfalls
- Releasing artifacts that compromise an active investigation
- One-and-done posting that fades instead of serialized, sustained coverage

**Tools:** YouTube, podcast distribution
