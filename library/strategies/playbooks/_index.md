# Playbooks

A playbook is an *ordered sequence* tuned to a recognizable case shape. Where pivots and patterns are building blocks, a playbook tells you which to run, in what order, for a specific starting situation — so you don't have to re-derive the sequence under pressure.

## Children
- **[[playbook-runaway-teen-social-first]]** — minor, likely voluntary, active social footprint. Confirm accounts → mine recent posts → triangulate friends → timeline → report. Heightened minor-safety bar throughout.
- **[[playbook-thin-selectors-name-city]]** — only a real name and a city. All disambiguation: combine the two, add inferred disambiguators, derive usernames, find a face, and avoid the people-search paywall. Set expectations honestly.
- **[[playbook-single-photo-unknown-subject]]** — one image, no identity. Reverse-image first, then parallel face + geolocation + EXIF/chronolocation, converging the three into a bootstrapped identity.

## How to use them
Match the case to a shape at triage (`[[phase-triage]]`). The playbook gives the order; the linked pivots/patterns give the how; the phases and antipatterns supply the guardrails (verification, opsec, don't force the match). A playbook is a starting sequence, not a script — deviate when the case tells you to, but know the default.
