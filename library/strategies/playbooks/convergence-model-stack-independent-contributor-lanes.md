# Convergence model: stack independent contributor lanes

> The Brandon Lawson resolution is attributed to the convergence of five independent contributor lanes: family persistence, volunteer data management (structured cell records), media/social amplification, professional investigation experience (PI liaison + drone imagery yielding 1000+ high-res photos), and coordinated systematic ground searches (10-member lane-pattern teams). The transferable playbook for a crowdsourced/hackathon case: do not rely on a single pivot - run parallel lanes (digital footprint, geospatial, amplification, expert/records, physical/ground) and look for the point where tw

**When to use:** On a stalled or thin-selector case where no single lead is conclusive and you can parallelize effort.

## Steps
- Open parallel lanes: digital footprint, geospatial/records, amplification, expert/PI/records liaison, and physical/ground search.
- Assign structured data ownership so raw leads become an auditable, geocoded record.
- Use amplification to recruit more analysts and witnesses into the lanes.
- Watch for cross-lane corroboration (e.g., tower cluster + drone imagery + ground search converging on one area).

## Signals it's working
- Two independent lanes point at the same location or associate
- Aerial/imagery analysis confirms a geospatial hypothesis

## Pitfalls
- Betting everything on one pivot and stalling when it dead-ends
- Running lanes in isolation without a shared structured record to spot corroboration

**Tools:** spreadsheet (Excel), drone imagery, YouTube
