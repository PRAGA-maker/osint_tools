# Plan the mission and define red lines before you start

> TOFM requires answering a structured set of questions before any searching: define the mission (what question, what specific info, what connection, what is out of scope), define success criteria (when does it end), set red lines that trigger stopping, and build a threat model. For missing-persons work this keeps the investigation focused on the find, prevents scope creep, and pre-commits you to stop and escalate if you stumble onto serious crime or it spills into the real world.

**When to use:** Before the first search of every case.

## Steps
- Define the mission: What question am I answering? What specific info do I need? What connection am I making? What is out of scope?
- Define success criteria: the investigation ends when the planned requirements are satisfied.
- Set red lines that trigger stopping: discovering evidence of a serious crime; encountering personally triggering material; the online investigation spilling into the real world; obsessing/spending too much time.
- Build a threat model: Who/what am I hiding from? Will the subject be monitoring? What might they do if they detect me? What are the consequences if my real identity is revealed?

## Signals it's working
- You can state in one sentence what would conclude the case and what would make you stop

## Pitfalls
- Endless scope creep with no defined end
- Continuing past a red line (e.g., evidence of a serious crime) instead of stopping and escalating
