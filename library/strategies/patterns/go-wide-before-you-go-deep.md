# Go wide before you go deep

> TOFM's pivoting guidance warns against diving into the first interesting thread. Map breadth first — surface all the accounts, links, and connections you can — before drilling deep into any one of them. This prevents premature rabbit-holing on a detail that turns out to be a dead end or the wrong person, and it gives you cross-corroboration material before you commit time.

**When to use:** Right after intake, whenever you have several candidate leads and feel the pull to chase the most exciting one.

## Steps
- Enumerate broadly and list every account/link/connection you find.
- Do a shallow pass on each to gauge relevance and identity confidence.
- Only after the landscape is mapped, pick the highest-confidence thread to investigate in depth.

## Signals it's working
- You can see how multiple leads corroborate or contradict each other before committing time

## Pitfalls
- Sinking hours into a single profile that later proves to be the wrong person
- Missing an obvious high-value account because you tunneled on a minor one
