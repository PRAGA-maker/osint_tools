# Passive reconnaissance only — never interact

> The single most important safety rule in TOFM: do passive reconnaissance exclusively and have NO interaction with anyone during an investigation. This explicitly bans DMs, emails, friend requests, and any interaction with social-media posts — not only the subject's, but anyone adjacent to the investigation. It also bans discussing the investigation outside safe channels. For live missing-persons cases this protects the subject, avoids tipping off bad actors, and keeps evidence admissible/ethical.

**When to use:** Continuously, for the entire duration of every investigation.

## Steps
- View, screenshot, and document — but never like, comment, follow, friend, or message.
- Apply the no-contact rule to associates and adjacent accounts, not just the subject.
- Discuss findings only in designated safe channels.
- If a step would require contact to proceed, stop and hand off rather than interact.

## Signals it's working
- You complete the entire investigation with zero outbound interactions

## Pitfalls
- Accidentally liking/following while browsing logged in
- Reaching out to a friend or family account 'just to confirm'
- Talking about the case in public channels
