# Desktop-research and public-records foundation

> Uncovered's Citizen Detective Guide frames the core skillset as diverse desktop research methods combined with knowing the components of requesting public information and records, and evaluating data-collection processes - blending publicly available information with crowdsourced insights to map a case before acting. The transferable baseline: before chasing social pivots, ground the case in systematic open-source desktop research and identify which public records (police reports, FOIA-style requests, court/property records) can be requested, so social-media leads are corroborated against auth

**When to use:** When building the corroborating records layer beneath social-media leads.

## Steps
- Map what public records exist for the case and how to request each.
- Run systematic desktop/open-source research on the subject and associates.
- Combine publicly available info with crowdsourced insights into one case map.
- Cross-check social-media findings against authoritative records before trusting them.

## Signals it's working
- A social-media lead is confirmed (or refuted) by a public record
- Records reveal addresses/associates that open new pivots

## Pitfalls
- Treating uncorroborated social posts as fact
- Not knowing the request pathway for records, leaving authoritative leads untapped

**Tools:** Uncovered Citizen Detective Guide, public records requests
