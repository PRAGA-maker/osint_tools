# Family-first rapid action before official report filing

> In the Brandon Lawson case study, the family began independent ground searches and pursued records within 24 hours because police delayed filing the missing-person report. The transferable pattern: do not wait for an official case number to begin collecting and preserving leads. The earliest hours are when social-media posts, geolocation pings, and witness memories are freshest and least likely to be deleted. For a hackathon investigator this means immediately snapshotting the subject's public profiles, last posts, and tagged content before they can be taken down.

**When to use:** Immediately upon receiving a fresh missing-persons case, before official channels are confirmed.

## Steps
- Snapshot/archive the subject's public social profiles and their most recent posts before deletion.
- Capture tagged photos, check-ins, and comments that establish last-known activity and associates.
- Begin a records/public-info request track in parallel with the digital capture.
- Build a timeline from the freshest digital traces while memories and posts are still live.

## Signals it's working
- You preserved posts/profiles that later get deleted or locked
- Last-activity timestamp narrows the search window

## Pitfalls
- Waiting for an official case to start collecting, losing volatile social evidence
- Failing to archive, so a privacy lockdown erases your leads
