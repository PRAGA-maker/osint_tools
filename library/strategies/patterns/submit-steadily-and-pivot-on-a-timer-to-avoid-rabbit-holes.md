# Submit steadily and pivot on a timer to avoid rabbit holes

> Investigators waste enormous time over-analyzing a single thread. The discipline that wins is a cadence rule: if you have not submitted a flag in ~10 minutes, stop and take a new direction. Pepper the board with valid small flags (tattoos, piercings, vehicles, substance-use indicators, Day-Last-Seen facts) without overthinking each one's significance, while respecting agreed quality standards. Consistent submission maintains momentum and forces you out of dead-end rabbit holes you would otherwise sink an hour into.

**When to use:** Throughout a time-boxed investigation to maintain throughput and avoid getting stuck.

## Steps
- Set a personal timer; if 10 minutes pass with no submission, pivot to a new angle or subject.
- Submit small valid flags as you find them rather than hoarding for a big find.
- Capture Day-Last-Seen facts about final photos/locations without overanalyzing.
- When a thread stalls, switch subjects or platforms instead of forcing it.

## Signals it's working
- Submissions are flowing every few minutes
- You catch yourself 15+ minutes into one thread with nothing to submit

## Pitfalls
- Sinking an hour into one rabbit hole
- Withholding small flags waiting for a perfect big one
- Monitoring the scoreboard instead of working
