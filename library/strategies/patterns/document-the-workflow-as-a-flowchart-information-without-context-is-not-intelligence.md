# Document the workflow as a flowchart: information without context is not intelligence

> The team's award-winning differentiator was not a single clever find but rigorous documentation. They built a flowchart (in draw.io) capturing every relevant intel point, the workflow connecting them, and side-by-side geolocation comparisons showing how each conclusion was sourced and confirmed. Their maxim: 'Information without context is not intelligence,' and proper documentation of intel points is 'just as important as the intel points themselves.' A clear, sourced, single-document narrative of how you got each answer is what elevates raw finds into intelligence and wins evaluation.

**When to use:** Throughout the investigation, and especially when assembling the final submission/report.

## Steps
- As you work, record each intel point with its source and how it was confirmed.
- Build a flowchart (e.g., draw.io) linking selectors, pivots, and conclusions.
- Include side-by-side comparisons for geolocation/identity confirmations.
- Present a single document that shows the full workflow, not just isolated findings.
- Ensure every claim has visible provenance so a reviewer can follow the chain.

## Signals it's working
- A reviewer can trace every conclusion back to its source from the document alone
- Geolocation/identity claims are backed by visible side-by-side comparisons

## Pitfalls
- Dumping raw findings without showing how they connect or were verified
- Leaving confirmations implicit, so the report reads as assertion rather than evidence

**Tools:** draw.io
