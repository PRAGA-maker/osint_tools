# Searchable optimization: put the subject's name in titles and cross-post

> Pappa documents that using the victim's name directly in video titles drove discovery via search, and that the Rachel Morin videos grew substantially with no established subscriber base because audiences found them through search and organic Facebook sharing across platforms (YouTube, TikTok, Facebook). The transferable, search-first lesson for OSINT pivots: a missing person's name is the highest-value search token; ensure case content is named and cross-posted so that anyone searching the name - including witnesses and the subject's own network - lands on your appeal, and so that your monitor

**When to use:** When optimizing case content for discovery and when setting up name-keyed monitoring across platforms.

## Steps
- Use the subject's full name as the primary search token in titles and captions.
- Cross-post the same content to YouTube, TikTok, and Facebook to maximize organic discovery.
- Rely on organic name-based search and sharing rather than paid promotion.
- Monitor the name across platforms to catch new third-party content and tips.

## Signals it's working
- Content grows from search/organic sharing without an existing audience
- Name search surfaces both your appeal and others' related posts

## Pitfalls
- Burying the name in generic titles, killing searchability
- Single-platform posting that misses where the relevant audience actually is

**Tools:** YouTube, Facebook, TikTok
