# Behaviorally-targeted authentic content over threat-framing

> FBI profiler Tim Pappa's framework for driving cold-case attention: content must align with investigative goals and target the people closest to a suspect. He warns of psychological reactance - when authorities describe a suspect with threatening labels ('serial killer', 'narcissistic'), people close to that person reject the characterization and withhold information because it contradicts their experience. Instead, use authentic, vulnerable, unscripted content. Audiences judge authenticity from behavioral samples under five minutes and reject 'surface acting'. The transferable OSINT-adjacent 

**When to use:** When crafting public appeals or content meant to elicit tips from people close to a subject or suspect.

## Steps
- Define the specific audience you want to act (those closest to the subject/suspect).
- Avoid threat-based or pathologizing language that triggers reactance and withholding.
- Use authentic, vulnerable, unscripted delivery instead of scripted statements.
- Keep key emotional/authentic moments concise (under ~5 minutes) since authenticity is judged fast.

## Signals it's working
- Tips arrive from people who personally knew the subject
- Comments show emotional resonance rather than defensiveness

## Pitfalls
- Vilifying language that causes the very people with information to shut down
- Over-produced 'surface acting' that audiences read as inauthentic
