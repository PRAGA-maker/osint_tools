# Persuasive geography: film/post from meaningful locations

> Pappa's 'persuasive geography' principle: recording content at locations significant to a case (where the person was last seen, where a crime occurred) triggers authentic cognitive and emotional responses that audiences perceive as genuine and share organically. For an investigator this doubles as a method to surface witnesses: posting geotagged content from the last-known location prompts locals and people familiar with the area to engage, comment, and supply place-specific knowledge that becomes new leads.

**When to use:** When you have a defined last-known location and want both authentic appeal content and location-specific witness tips.

## Steps
- Identify the location significant to the case (last-seen point, found site, route).
- Create or solicit content anchored to that location for authentic emotional response.
- Geotag/name the location so locals and familiar parties surface in engagement.
- Mine the resulting comments and shares for place-specific witness leads.

## Signals it's working
- Locals comment with area-specific knowledge
- Engagement clusters geographically around the last-known location

## Pitfalls
- Posting from a location while an active scene is being processed
- Treating location content only as appeal and ignoring the witness leads it generates
